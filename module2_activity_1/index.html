Flood Control

Flooding remains one of the biggest environmental challenges in the Philippines, especially in Metro Manila and low-lying areas. The government has invested heavily in flood control projects such as pumping stations, dikes, and drainage system upgrades. While these projects aim to reduce flooding during typhoon season, concerns have been raised about delays, mismanagement, and possible corruption in contract bidding.

The Discayas

The "Discayas" issue refers to a recent controversy that sparked nationwide discussion on governance and accountability. It became a trending topic across social media platforms and news outlets, highlighting concerns about leadership, transparency, and the role of citizens in demanding answers from those in power.

In interviews aired from September 2024 to January 2025, veteran broadcasters Julius Babao and Korina Sanchez featured Sarah and Curlee Discaya—owners of major contractor firms like Alpha & Omega Gen., St. Timothy Construction, and St. Gerrard Construction—which have been named among top recipients of Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH) flood control contracts.

In Babao’s “Unplugged” YouTube segment titled “Ate Sarah at Kuya Curlee, Dating Mahirap na Naging Bilyonaryo!”, Sarah Discaya told how their construction business improved significantly after getting DPWH projects.

In one interview, she openly discussed buying a luxury Rolls-Royce—one of about 40 luxury cars she and her husband own—and explained it by pointing out a feature she liked (“payong” mechanism) rather than utility.

The Korina Sanchez interview, which has since been removed from official channels, similarly portrayed Sarah’s “asenso” (rise) from humble beginnings—from parents working as OFWs, to her growing success in construction, with public attention to their personal lifestyle.

These interviews have triggered criticism because the Discaya-owned firms are implicated in the so-called ghost flood control projects—projects that are non-existent, incomplete, or poorly constructed. Pasig City Mayor Vico Sotto has questioned whether these interviews amount to paid placements intended to polish public image, particularly since they involve contractors entering politics and allegedly benefiting from DPWH contracts.

The media teams denied these are paid promos, maintaining the features are lifestyle or success-stories chosen for public interest, not investigative reports.

The controversy thus raises issues of media ethics, transparency, and the overlap among wealth display, political ambition, and public trust, particularly in contexts where public infrastructure funds are under scrutiny for misuse.

Trillion Peso March

The Trillion Peso March is a massive protest movement that emerged in response to issues of government corruption and misuse of public funds. It symbolizes the growing frustration of Filipino citizens over unaccounted expenditures, pork barrel misuse, and the lack of transparency in budget allocation. The event reflects the people's demand for accountability and justice in the handling of taxpayers’ money.

Corruption in the Philippines

Corruption continues to be one of the most pressing issues in the Philippines. It affects government services, economic growth, and public trust. Various advocacy groups and citizens are calling for stronger anti-corruption measures, better implementation of laws, and a culture of integrity among public officials.
